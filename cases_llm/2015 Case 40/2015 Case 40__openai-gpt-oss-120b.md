### 1. Hypertensive Emergency versus Severe Uncontrolled Hypertension  

#### 1.1 Definition and Clinical Correlates  

A hypertensive emergency is defined by **severe elevation of blood pressure (usually SBP > 180 mm Hg or DBP > 120 mm Hg) together with evidence of acute target‑organ damage**. #### 1.2 Evidence Supporting Hypertensive Emergency  

- **BP magnitude**: SBP > 200 mm Hg is strongly associated with end‑organ stress.

- **Obesity and chronic stress**: Both are risk factors for malignant hypertension.
